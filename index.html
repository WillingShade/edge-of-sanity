EDGE OF SANITY

A psychological horror experience set within a fractured reality. You are not losing your mind. You are what remains of it.

THE WORLD

Reality is broken. Buildings float. Trees grow upside down. The sky is torn open by glowing rifts. What begins as a normal workplace slowly reveals itself as something deeply wrong. The world degrades alongside the mind that contains it.

THE PREMISE

You work shifts at a quiet asylum. You check on patients. You clean, organize paperwork, and perform routine tasks. Between shifts, you return to your apartment, listening to the radio, writing in your journal.

Over time, the world fractures. Notes become incoherent. Broadcasts dissolve into noise. Patients begin to change. At the climax, the truth is revealed: you are trapped within your own mind, the last fragment of a shattered sanity.

THE EMOTIONS

Joy

A smiling man whose happiness grows grotesque. Laughter becomes uncontrollable. Joy turns predatory.

Fear

Constant anxiety that escalates into terror. Shrieking, panic, and overwhelming dread.

Sadness

A quiet despair that weighs on the world itself. The environment bends beneath its sorrow.

Hope

Never seen. Only heard through calls, notes, and messages. When Hope is gone, nothing holds the world together.

DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

Edge of Sanity is built as a slow-burn psychological descent. There are no cheap scares. Horror emerges from routine, attachment, and loss. Visuals, sound, and narrative degrade together, creating an experience that feels unstable, fragmented, and deeply personal.

Description:
Edge of Sanity is a slow-burn psychological horror game about routine, emotional decay, and fractured reality. Set within a shifting asylum that exists inside a broken mind, players perform mundane tasks while the world, its inhabitants, and their own perception unravel.

Key Features:

  • Emotion-driven NPCs that evolve into threats
  • Environmental storytelling and reality fragmentation
  • Routine-based gameplay that turns unsettling over time
  • Minimalist horror with no reliance on jump scares
  • Atmospheric audio and visual degradation tied to narrative
